Speedtest by Ookla
speedtest.net
Industry standard, 25+ years
Open Landing Page View Test Result

● What They Do Well

  • Iconic GO button with zero-friction single-click start STEAL
  • Animated needle gauge during test gives satisfying visual feedback of speed climbing
  • Color-coded phases: cyan for download, magenta for upload. Clear visual state distinction STEAL
  • Phase indicators (4 dots below ping) show test progress stages at a glance
  • ISP + Server context always visible: you know who you're testing against
  • Shareable result IDs with permalink URLs
  • Ping/Jitter/Packet Loss shown alongside speed (3 latency values)

● What They Do Poorly

  • No bufferbloat measurement: the biggest blind spot. Only shows idle latency, not latency under load GAP
  • Results page is cluttered with CTAs: "Create Account", "Rate Your Provider", "Find ISP" ads
  • No historical trend view without an account. Results are ephemeral
  • No service rate context: 944 Mbps means nothing without knowing if you're paying for 1 Gbps or 2 Gbps WE CAN DO BETTER
  • No utilization percentage: "am I getting what I pay for?" is unanswered
  • Gauge is decorative during results: static display, wasted screen real estate
  • Dark theme only, no accessibility options

Fast.com (Netflix)
fast.com
Simplicity-first, consumer-focused
Open Fast.com (auto-starts test)

● What They Do Well

  • Auto-start on page load: zero clicks needed. Fastest time-to-value of any speedtest STEAL THIS CONCEPT
  • Massive typography: the speed number is impossible to miss. Bold hierarchy
  • Progressive disclosure: simple number first, "Show more info" reveals latency + upload STEAL
  • Unloaded vs Loaded latency distinction: shows bufferbloat impact implicitly
  • Data usage shown (2800MB DL, 2170MB UL): transparency about test cost
  • Ultra clean layout: no clutter, no ads, no upsells

● What They Do Poorly

  • No visual progress indicator beyond a spinner. No gauge, no chart, no phase info
  • No history at all: completely stateless, no trend data
  • No bufferbloat grade: shows unloaded/loaded but doesn't interpret the delta WE CAN DO BETTER
  • No server selection: can't control test parameters
  • Bland design: white text on dark grey, no personality, no brand expression
  • No utilization or service rate context
  • Light on details: no jitter, no packet loss, no percentile data

● What They Do Well

  • Bufferbloat grade (A-F) as the hero metric, not speed. This is the right priority STEAL

  • Affiliate product links below results: undermines credibility as a diagnostic tool
  • No progress visualization during test: just a progress bar, no gauge or chart
  • No historical comparison: single-test view only
  • Speed is secondary: DL/UL numbers are small relative to the grade. For ISP context we need both prominently BALANCE
  • No service rate utilization: same gap as everyone else
  • Color-coded borders are confusing: green, yellow, cyan borders around sections lack clear meaning
  • Box plots are niche: most consumers won't understand percentile distributions SIMPLIFY

Design Principles for SmartOS Speedtest Page

Our unique advantage: we run on the router itself, we know the service rate, we have BBST with bufferbloat grading, and we have historical data. No competitor has all of these.

01

Speed + Quality, Equal Weight

Hero the speed results AND the bufferbloat grade together. Ookla ignores quality. Waveform buries speed. We show both prominently because ISP subscribers care about both: "am I getting what I pay for?" AND "why do my video calls lag?"

02

Service Rate Context (Our Killer Feature)

Show % utilization against the provisioned plan. 944 Mbps on a 1 Gbps plan = 94% utilization = great. 944 Mbps on an 8 Gbps plan = 12% = problem. Nobody else can do this because they don't know your plan speed. We do.

03

Progressive Disclosure

Simple view: big speed numbers, grade pill, utilization bar. One-click "details" reveals latency breakdown, jitter, percentiles, server info. Steal from fast.com's layered approach, not Cloudflare's data dump.

04

Animated Test Phase Feedback

Combine Ookla's gauge satisfaction with Cloudflare's real-time throughput chart. Show the speed climbing in real-time with clear phase indicators (Idle Latency, Download, Upload). Color-code each phase distinctly.

05

Historical Trend (Our Other Killer Feature)

We archive up to 100 results. Show a speed history chart with bufferbloat grades overlaid. "Your connection has improved since Tuesday" or "Bufferbloat worsened after firmware update." Nobody else offers on-device history.

06

Practical Impact Translation

Steal Waveform's capability matrix but make it better. "Your connection supports: 4K streaming (yes), video calls (yes), cloud gaming (limited by bufferbloat)." Turn technical numbers into answers people actually want.
